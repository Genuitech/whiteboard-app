# COMC Autopilot Policy Sheet (Hands-Off Model)

**Owner:** Chea  
**Operator:** Dexter  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Objective:** Run a no-shipping, no-grading, COMC-only card business focused on consistent cashflow and controlled risk.

---

## 1) Operating Constraints (Non-Negotiable)

- Platform scope: **COMC only**
- No physical handling by owner (no packing, no shipping)
- No grading submissions
- No off-platform inventory intake unless explicitly approved

---

## 2) Capital Allocation Rules

- **Starting bankroll:** $5,000
- **Capital deployment cap:** 85% max deployed at any time
- **Cash reserve:** 15% minimum always held
- **Max position per card:** 2.5% of bankroll (=$125 at $5k)
- **Max exposure per player/team/IP/set:** 15% of bankroll (=$750 at $5k)
- **Max daily new deployment:** 20% of bankroll (=$1,000 at $5k)

---

## 3) Buy Filters (Must Pass All)

A card is eligible only if all conditions are true:

1. **Liquidity:** At least 3 recent sold comps in reasonable time window
2. **Spread:** Current ask is at least 20% below normalized recent sale value
3. **Net margin floor:** Estimated net margin after COMC fees ≥ 18%
4. **Price band:** Buy price between $5 and $125 (unless overridden)
5. **No hype spike chase:** Avoid cards with abrupt unsustained price spikes
6. **No concentrated duplicate risk:** Max 3 copies of same exact card unless velocity justifies

### Preferred segments
- Modern stars with steady liquidity
- Popular Pokémon singles with active transaction volume
- Mid-price cards with frequent turnover vs thin high-end inventory

### Avoid segments
- Ultra-illiquid niche inserts with sparse sales
- Extremely low-value cards where fees erode margin
- News/hype-only spikes with no comp stability

---

## 4) Pricing Framework

### Initial listing price
Use normalized fair value from recent solds:

- **Target list price:** 95% to 102% of normalized fair value
- If high velocity: price for faster turnover (95–98%)
- If lower velocity but strong spread: price 99–102%

### Auto markdown schedule (time-based)
- Day 0: Initial list price
- Day 14: -4%
- Day 30: -6% from current
- Day 45: -8% from current
- Day 60: Move to **Exit Queue** unless margin remains strong and liquidity confirmed

---

## 5) Sell-Through and Inventory Buckets

Each card must be categorized:

1. **Quick Flip** (target 7–30 days)
2. **Standard Turn** (target 30–60 days)
3. **Exit Queue** (aged >60 days or thesis broken)

### Portfolio targets
- Quick Flip: 50–60%
- Standard Turn: 30–40%
- Exit Queue: <10%

---

## 6) Risk Controls / Kill Switches

Pause all new buys immediately if any trigger occurs:

1. 14-day sell-through rate falls below 8%
2. Realized net ROI (rolling 30 days) drops below 3%
3. Cash reserve falls below 10%
4. Any single category/player exceeds exposure cap

### Recovery protocol
- Stop new purchases for 7 days
- Aggressively markdown aged inventory
- Rebuild reserve to 15%
- Resume only when sell-through and ROI recover to thresholds

---

## 7) Performance Targets

### Minimum acceptable
- Monthly net ROI on deployed capital: **4%+
- Inventory turn window:** ≤60 days average

### Good execution
- Monthly net ROI on deployed capital: **8%+
- Average turn window:** 30–45 days

### Stretch
- Monthly net ROI: 10–15% with stable risk controls

---

## 8) Weekly Operating Rhythm

### Monday
- Refresh comp baselines
- Scan for new buy opportunities
- Enforce exposure caps

### Wednesday
- Reprice and apply markdown schedule
- Move stale cards toward Exit Queue

### Friday
- Weekly P/L review
- Sell-through and aging report
- Recommend budget reallocation by segment

---

## 9) KPI Dashboard (Track Weekly)

1. Starting bankroll
2. Deployed capital
3. Cash reserve %
4. New buys count + cost
5. Sales count + gross proceeds
6. Fees paid
7. Net profit (weekly + month-to-date)
8. Net ROI on deployed capital
9. Sell-through rate (7-day / 14-day / 30-day)
10. Inventory aging buckets (0–30 / 31–60 / 61+ days)
11. Top winners / top laggards
12. Exposure by player/team/IP/set

---

## 10) Approval Matrix (Hands-Off Guardrails)

Dexter can execute autonomously when all rules are met.

Requires owner approval only when:
- Single card buy > $125
- Any exception to buy filters
- Changing risk limits or exposure caps
- Deploying beyond 85% of bankroll
